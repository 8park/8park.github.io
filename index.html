My Path: From Engineer to Educator

My interest in education grew naturally from my experiences as an engineer and leader. Each step of my journey revealed a new piece of the puzzle.

Chapter 1: The Foundation in Precision

Prototyping Technician

My journey started with a fascination for turning ideas into reality. In the demanding automotive R&D world, I learned the non-negotiable value of precision. This purely technical role taught me that getting the details right is the foundation of any successful project.

Precision Engineering CAD/CAM Prototyping

Chapter 2: The Addition of Leadership

Artillery Maintenance Team Leader

Leading a 4-technician maintenance team taught me that technical skill alone isn't enough. I had to not only solve problems but also train my team to do the same. This was my first real taste of teaching ‚Äì the challenge of transferring complex knowledge clearly and effectively under pressure.

Team Leadership Process Management Technical Training

Chapter 3: The Spark of Education

University Lab Technician & Instructor

This role was a turning point where my passion for hands-on education truly flourished. While managing a lab of over 50 machines, I took on two major educational initiatives.

First, I developed and taught more than 10 foundational engineering workshops to over 100 students, covering topics like SolidWorks basics, 3D printing, and building simple electronics.

In addition, to ensure the lab's long-term operational continuity, I produced an extensive video training library of over 20 modules for future staff, documenting everything from equipment maintenance to software procedures.

This, combined with leading a student team to build a robot arm and mentoring startups, solidified my passion: empowering people by building accessible, hands-on educational systems.

3D Printing Robotics Drones VR Curriculum Development Mentorship
View the Training Video Library

Chapter 4: The Confirmation

AI Researcher at UTRGV

As part of a USDA research project, I played a key role in the early stages of developing a honey bee monitoring system. My contributions included hardware redesign, creating a remote video recording script for data collection, labeling the collected data, and writing the initial code to train a bee detection model using TensorFlow on a Jetson Nano. This experience confirmed my ability to apply AI to solve real-world problems from the ground up.

Computer Vision Embedded Systems Data Pipeline TensorFlow
View Research Project View the Paper

Chapter 5: The Lesson in Grassroots Operations

Retail Store Launch Team

Beyond engineering and code, I wanted to understand how a large-scale business operates from the ground up. Participating in the launch of a new Burlington store was a real-world MBA lesson. From organizing the warehouse to setting up the sales floor and finally serving the first customers, I saw firsthand how every detail, from logistics to teamwork under pressure, directly impacts the bottom line and customer satisfaction. It was a powerful reminder that successful systems, whether in technology or retail, are built on well-executed fundamentals.

Retail Operations Logistics Teamwork

Chapter 6: The Synthesis

Founder & Developer of FinitUp

FinitUp is my ongoing project to translate business theory into a tangible product. The goal is to automate the valuation models I learned in my MBA finance classes using AI, aiming to reduce analysis time by 90% compared to manual methods. This is my 'Learning by Building' philosophy in action, merging my entire skillset to create value.

I'm building upon the open-source work of the AI4Finance Foundation (FinRL, FinRobot) to implement automated analysis, recommendations, and virtual trading.

Python FinRL Generative AI
