My Story: From South Korea to South Texas

My journey began in South Korea, where I was always drawn to the role of a leader—someone who improves their environment and helps their peers. This passion led me from leading student groups in my youth, to leading a technical team in the military, to mastering engineering in the competitive automotive supply chain. A desire for growth brought me to the U.S. and UTRGV, where I deepened my expertise in Computer Science and AI, and am now honing my business acumen through an MBA. This unique path has forged my mission: to weave these diverse threads of technology, leadership, and education together to create new value.

My Role: Researcher

Problem: The USDA needed a way to monitor honeybee populations in real-time to study environmental impacts.
Contribution: As a researcher, I contributed to the development of a real-time AI/IoT monitoring system using Jetson Nano and TensorFlow. My work involved fabricating custom hardware enclosures, installing the camera network, and assisting with the computer vision models.

My Teaching Philosophy

Fostering Creative Problem-Solving Through an Interdisciplinary Approach

My educational goal is to lower the barrier to entry for technology, helping students feel comfortable and confident. To achieve this, I connect complex principles to real-world examples by drawing from my interdisciplinary knowledge in Computer Science (M.S.) and Mechanical Engineering (B.Eng.). Furthermore, I lead a hands-on, practical approach using modern tools like AI, CAD design, and 3D printing, enabling students to gain a sense of accomplishment by bringing their ideas to life. Through this process, students will grow into creative talents who can identify problems on their own and integrate diverse technologies to create original solutions.
